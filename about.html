About us.

Off road vehicles are stuck in the past. We’re here to change that.

The era of software-defined vehicles is opening up new horizons in performance, efficiency, safety and driver enjoyment. But to get there, we must fundamentally rethink how vehicles perceive the world around them. That’s where we come in. At Potential, we’re combining computer vision, AI models and software engineering to enable off-road vehicles to see, interpret and proactively prepare for complex terrain and surface conditions. The result? The next generation in ADAS, designed to keep drivers moving even when the going gets toughest off road and on it.

It all started when Sam Poirier and his friends saved a Subaru Impreza from the scrapyard and converted it into an electric vehicle.

“I wanted everyone and their grandparents to step in and be able to instantly drive like professional rally drivers. Software defined vehicles allow you to build systems to make that happen.”

Potential was born in 2018 to unlock expert level off road driving abilities for everyone. How? By repurposing forward facing on vehicle sensors such as cameras, radar and lidar to enable vehicles to see and recognise terrain ahead, and autonomously and pre emptively adjust vehicle settings based on data computed in real time, on vehicle, and without GPS or maps.

We unveiled our vision for the future of off roading in 2022 in the shape of Adventure 1, a real life design concept that showcased how our software technologies could work in production type vehicle.

In 2023, we revealed our core on vehicle perception and computation technology, called Terrain Intelligence, and our suite of automated driver aids and vehicle controls, known as Off Road OS.

Today, we’re working with some of the world’s best known companies across the automotive, powersports, defense and mining sectors, helping to make their vehicles and drivers more capable, more confident and safer in all conditions.

Who we are.

We’re an interdisciplinary team that includes engineers, data scientists, computer vision specialists, and experts in AI and machine learning. We split our time between our purpose-built off-road test track and our lab (in Fredericton, NB, Canada), and flexible working arrangements that let us live our best adventures.

Real world testing.

Alongside leading-edge software modelling and simulation tools, we conduct extensive real-world development at our very own off-road test track, which we call The Sandbox. We’ve also built two sophisticated off-road test vehicles, which feature individual wheel motors, active suspension, and individually-selectable forward-facing sensors (including cameras, radar and lidar), allowing us to test virtually any hardware configuration for our customers.
